Sales Intelligence Portal

Interactive visual analytics of sales data across regions, representatives, products, and time.

Graph 01
Donut Chart: Revenue by Rep
Each arc segment represents one sales representative's share of total revenue. The inner radius creates a donut form, making it easy to compare proportions.
Hover over any slice for exact revenue figures
Key Insight Kivell and Andrews together account for the largest combined revenue share, driven by high-value Desk and Binder orders.
Graph 02
Streamgraph: Revenue Over Time
The stacked area chart reveals how each product's total revenue ebbed and flowed across months. Wider bands signal higher sales volume.
Scroll-wheel to zoom · Click-drag to pan
Key Insight Binder sales show strong cyclical peaks — especially in late 2021 and late 2022. Desk items create sharp spikes due to high unit costs.
Graph 03
Trellis Histogram: Unit Distribution
Each small panel shows the frequency distribution of order quantities. The red vertical line marks the mean order size.
Hover over any bar to highlight its frequency
Key Insight Pencil and Binder have highly spread distributions. Desk orders cluster tightly at very low quantities (1–10 units).
Graph 04
Bar Chart: Regional Revenue
Compares total revenue generated across East, Central, and West sales regions.
Click a bar to highlight that region
Key Insight Central region dominates in total revenue, reflecting the highest concentration of sales reps and volume.
Graph 05
Scatter Plot: Efficiency Correlation
Each point is a transaction. This reveals whether expensive items sell in low quantities and cheap items in bulk.
Scroll to zoom · Drag to pan · Hover for details
Key Insight Clear negative correlation: Desk and Pen Set items cluster at high cost + low units. Pencils cluster at very low cost with high unit counts.
